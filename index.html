why Jakarta

Jakarta, the unknown metropolitan city

Jakarta's story is one of colonial conquest, resistance, and transformation. Originally known as Jayakarta, the city's strategic location attracted Dutch colonizers in the 16th century, who renamed it Batavia and established it as a trading hub. Under Dutch rule, Batavia flourished but also faced unrest, including the infamous 1740 massacre. However, it became a center of resistance during the nationalist movements of the early 20th century, ultimately leading to Indonesia's independence in 1945, with Jakarta as its capital.
Since independence, Jakarta has undergone rapid urbanization and development, becoming a sprawling metropolis of over 10 million people. Despite its challenges, such as traffic congestion and pollution, Jakarta remains the heart of Indonesia, reflecting the country's rich history, diverse culture, and ongoing evolution as a dynamic global city.

Restaurants you should visit in Jakarta

Padang Merdeka

The one stop place if you want to try authentic West Sumatran food. Nasi Padang is a traditional Indonesian dish from West Sumatra. It features steamed rice served with a variety of flavorful side dishes like rendang, fried chicken, and sambal. Known for its rich flavors and aromatic spices, it's a popular choice both in Indonesia and globally.


Address

Jl. Cipete Raya No. 1C, Fatmawati, South Jakarta


Specialty

West Sumatran food also known as "Nasi Padang" in Indonesian.
